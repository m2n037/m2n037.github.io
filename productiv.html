Daily Schedule

A compilation on productivity techniques for knowledge workers

Background

I was a terrible procrastinator with actually a lot of responsibilities that included: working full-time, teaching part-time, spending time with SO, doing research on my own for gradschool application. I read books, was motivated, did something about it and fell back to my old habits in a week or so.

tl;dr version of the method

  • Start small but keep your eyes on the big picture

  • Minimize context switching

  • Plan your commute and stupid time wasters

  • Make it a ritual. Your brain will resist you first, and then plead you to do it

  • Have time for a alone break

  • Don't try to read hacker news and find solution to your homework in the same time

  • Have time scheduled for every little thing

  • Remember that executing tasks is not the point, getting into a habbit is.

Explanations

  • Morning Ritual – I wake up naturally now, but I did use alarms. I wake up at 0500 - 0515 and immediately hit the shower. By 0530, I am refreshed and don't have a hint of morning lethargy. You can read about it here, a technique by Shaolin monks. I get refreshed with a cup of green tea with peanuts/oats/cereals, and respond to calls of nature in 1520 minutes. I listen to a playlist [playlist at soundcloud] that has a upbeat rythm. I get ready for running by latest 0600, and go running for 20 minutes. I come back at 0620 do Yoga for 1020 minutes depending on available time, then get ready for work. All my stuff is already in bag, the dress and shoes already kept in a particular place. I am at the busstop by 0645. I rigorously follow the aforementioned schedule, but remain flexible on the time spent on it.

  • Commuting – I read books in the kindle during commuting. I take calls, but don't make any calls except to my family or SO.

  • Morning work ritual - I* open my PC, check all mails, send the interesting things in RSS feed (mostly automated in Inoreader), and modify my daily agenda. I use a text file plan.txt to write down all my priority goals for that day and plan-2.txt to write down errands (paying bills, recharge my mother's phone etc.). This daily agenda is usually done the previous day, but changed according to DSBGM's direction and meetings. I also have a random.txt/ to write down any recurring/important thought during deliberate practice. I go for a cup of tea with Vinay who just reaches work. While coming back to desk, I fill up my water bottle.

  • Timeblock (Deliberate Practice) - I take an item from agenda, work according to forest app (45/5 pomodoro technique), and don't allow any distractions. Firefox/Chrome blocks most time-sucking websites, and IE is the browser I can use to browse facebook. I drink a lot of water during this time. NO DISTRACTIONS ALLOWED.

  • Break I - I check mail again, read 1/2 interesting articles. I take calls, so if you want to talk to me, this is the best time to get a proper response.

  • Lunch time - I *go for lunch with Vinayak or Tarun and usually eat alone. I will walk from Kabini Canteen to Nano Science department and come back to CPDM through the main building route. I take the less travelled road, not to be disturbed.

  • Shutdown Ritual - I close all work related windows at 1635 and update my daily agenda for the next day. I update my guide about my progress before this, and talk to Vinay once. I repeat that my work is done for the day and I am going home now. I don't have to worry about it anymore. I also write an accomplishment journal for the day and wind off. I reach the bustop by 4.45pm.

  • Family and Friends - I keep two hours for this. Then an hour for errands, which I batch process in winderlist app.

  • Unschedule is for nothing. That is my alone time, movie time, and alone dancing time.

  • Night ritual - I check my mail, clear my inbox, call my SO once and meditate for 5 minutes before drifting off to sleep. I usually listen to a playlist during that time.

I complete my day and sleep like a child for around 6 hours
