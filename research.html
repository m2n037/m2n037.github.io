Research Synopsis

The broad area of interest for my research is locomotion, manipulation, and grasping in Robotics. I pursued a project on building a completely mechanical, single-DOF, steerable hexapod which led to a strong interest in this area.

BUG - A Single DOF Steerable Mechanical Hexapod

BUG - Elevator Speech:

This project is an exploration of the capability of mechanical components for complex actuation and control. The trajectory of human foot is similar to the alphabet D, rotated anticlockwise 90 degrees. A similar coupler curve can be found in the Hoeken's linkage. An optimized mechanism (in terms of number of links and coupler curve) is designed as a functioning leg and six of them is connected to a rectangular frame with a tripod gait. A single rotating motion is connected to the links through chain drives to control all the legs in a particular gait. The vehicle can be steered using a mechanism that facilitates differential stride for opposing legs.

BUG - Project Details

Advisor: Dr. Dibakar Sen, Associate Professor, Dept of Mechanical Engineering and CPDM, IISc Bangalore.
Project Team: Me, Lalit Singhal
Project Duration: 2012-present

This project exposed me to many different areas of control, dynamics, and simulation. A few salient points:

  • Advanced mechanism design

  • Human locomotion

  • Gait analysis

  • Control simplification using mechanical components intelligently

  • Complexity of prototyping mechanism

  • Deficiency of simulations to predict workable prototype

  • Importance of design for manufacturing
